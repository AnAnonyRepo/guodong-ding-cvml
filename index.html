About

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Computer Vision & Machine Learning (CVML) Lab, National University of Singapore, working on the frontiers of video understanding and human action analysis. My research focuses on enabling machines to parse complex, temporally extended human activities from videos — with implications for robotics, healthcare, and industrial automation.

My primary research thrust is Temporal Action Segmentation: how to accurately decompose untrimmed procedural videos into meaningful action segments. I have published extensively at top venues including CVPR, NeurIPS, ECCV, and IEEE TPAMI, contributing both foundational analyses and novel frameworks (online, incremental, semi-supervised) for this challenging problem.

Beyond action segmentation, I am passionate about medical video analysis (coronary angiography, surgical workflow), dataset efficiency via generative models, and multimodal reasoning. I believe elegant models with strong temporal inductive biases are the key to robust video intelligence.
